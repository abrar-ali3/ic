  • About

    The Illuminated Cities Project seeks to address racial, religious, socioeconomic, and physical segregation that youth face as obstacles to becoming self-actualized contributors towards a more socially just and compassionate world.

    Who We Are: The Illuminated Cities Project is a youth empowerment program that aims to utilize international travel as a catalyst for youth development. We are focused on working with high school aged students and have three key foci to inspire our youth towards social innovation: creative arts, sustainable development, and peace/reconciliation through athletics. Through a twelve-month fellowship program, Illuminated will provide students the skills and support they need to develop a plan to improve the conditions of their community. By bringing together youth from racially, religiously, socio-economically, and geographically different places, Illuminated will create a network of resources for students who can share insights and work together as social innovators for their communities. One month of the fellowship will be spent abroad so that fellows can meet other young people from around the globe to see how they are improving their communities. The program will culminate when youth return to the U.S. and put their plans into practice that Illuminated will continue to support and evaluate with them.

  • About

    Vision:Illuminated Cities envisions a world where interdependence and exchange of ideas serve to make the world a socially just place. By getting young social innovators to interact with more experienced change-agents in international communities, a collective process can take place where the youth are able to address social challenges on a local level with inspiration and best practices from international models.

    Mission: The Illuminated Cities Project seeks to give young people, especially those in communities affected by segregation, an opportunity for leadership, self-development, and community-development in an international context. Fellows in the program build life skills, develop an Action Plan for their home-community, and are partnered with a mentor and a local organization to help them implement their Action Plan on the ground level. The fellows will be social innovators for their home-communities, creating a cycle of revitalization and development.

    Target Population: In the United States, our target population is youth in communities affected by segregation, especially Detroit, Chicago, and New York City, three of America’s most segregated cities. As an overall project, however, we aim to bridge gaps and inspire action through evoking dialogue among youth in global contexts.

  • Pilot Project

    During December 22-29, 2012, the Illuminated Cities Project launched its pilot project. Our team was comprised of one suburban fellow (Alina Raza), one urban fellow (Yaqueline Rodriguez), a youth documentarian (Shaiz Raza), an adult ally (Xian Barrett), and the project director (Zehra Imam).

    Learn More About The Fellows In This Video!

    Prior to traveling to Mexico our team engaged in conversations about cultural sensitivity and culture shock in the American and global contexts. Fellows did reflections and tele-conferenced with one another about their lives and experiences with education.

  • Mexico City

    We had workshops on:
    - Sustainability for self and community
    -­ Action Plans for our fellows
    ‐ Speaker‐Listener series to get to know one another and others in the Mexican context

    We met with faith communities to learn about culture, interfaith exchange of ideas, and fellowship:
    -­ In the Mexican Christian community, we went to church for Christmas mass and had dinner with a family.
    - In the Muslim community, we observed/participated in Friday prayers, and met with local community members for lunch.

    We also met with social entrepreneurs in the Mexican context so that our youth could gain an understanding of how they could implement their projects through individuals such as Jorge Olmos and organizations such as Espirit du Campeon. We discovered how artists are using creative arts as a medium for social change through organizations such as Danceability-Mexico. We worked with our team on creating and building partnerships in the Mexican context as well as with one another to build a sustainable network of support upon return from the program.

    1.Majora Carter: As part of the Illuminated Cities' advisory board, Majora helps the team with bringing its vision to life. Carter, who was awarded a 2005 MacArthur "genius" grant, served as executive director of Sustainable South Bronx for 7 years, where she pushed both for eco-friendly practices (such as green and cool roofs) and, equally important, job training and green-related economic development for her vibrant neighborhood on the rise. Since leaving SSBx in 2008, Carter has formed the economic consulting and planning firm the Majora Carter Group, to bring her pioneering approach to communities far outside the South Bronx. Carter is working within the cities of New Orleans, Detroit and the small coastal towns of Northeastern North Carolina. The Majora Carter Group is putting the green economy and green economic tools to use, unlocking the potential of every place -- from urban cities and rural communities, to universities, government projects, businesses and corporations -- and everywhere else in between.
